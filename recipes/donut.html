Donut Recipe

Go Back to Index

Description:

These homemade donuts are soft, fluffy, and perfectly golden brown. Easy to make, they're ideal for breakfast or any occasion. Customize them with a glaze, powdered sugar, or your favorite filling for a delicious, melt-in-your -mouth treat!

For an extra touch, try adding a sprinkle of cinnamon sugar or dipping them in chocolate ganache. Whether you fry or bake them, these donuts are sure to impress with their light texture and irresistible flavor. Perfect for sharing or enjoying all on your own!

Ingredients Required:

  • Milk
  • Yeast
  • Sugar
  • Eggs
  • Butter
  • Salt and Vanilla Extract
  • Nutmeg
  • Flour

Steps to make donut:

  1. Prepare the dough. The dough comes together with a mixer. You can also make the dough by hand, but it requires a bit of arm muscle. After the dough comes together in the mixing bowl, knead it for 5-7 minutes.
  2. Let the dough rise. In a relatively warm environment, the dough rises in about 90 minutes. Then punch down the dough to release the air.
  3. Roll & cut into doughnuts. Roll the dough out to 1/2-inch thickness. Cut the doughnuts using a 3-3.5-inch doughnut cutter. Line 1 or 2 baking sheets with parchment paper or a silicone baking mat. Place doughnuts (and doughnut holes!) onto the lined baking sheet(s), then lightly cover and allow to rest for 30 minutes as you heat the oil.
  4. Prepare the oil. Using a heavy-bottomed pot or Dutch oven and an oil thermometer, heat vegetable oil to 375°F (191°C).
  5. Fry the doughnuts. Working with 2-3 doughnuts at a time, cook for 1 minute on each side. Carefully remove from the oil and place onto prepared rack. Repeat with remaining doughnuts. (See my recipe note about making the doughnut holes.)

Making the glaze for the donut:

  1. Make a simple 3-ingredient glaze: just milk or cream, confectioners' sugar, and vanilla extract.
  2. Whisk the 3 ingredients together. Dunk each side of the warm doughnuts into the glaze. The glaze will set in about 20 minutes.
